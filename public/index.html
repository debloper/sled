What is it?

Sled.IO* is a proof of concept.
It's a prototype to demonstrate an idea in its simplest possible form.
It's a projectile-rocket-launcher in disguise, that you can comically pull out of your pocket.

It releases developers from the pain of implementating various identity/authentication methods.
It gives users the freedom of choice to choose their favorite auth-provider.
Sled.IO* harnesses & abstracts other auth-mechanisms.

It's a customizable, distributed, drop-in, authetication gateway.
You may want to know more about the origin & design of the platform.
* for the record, the platform that empowers Sled.IO was started with a codename: "Rhesus".

How to get started?

For a webapp, just have the following bit of code in your HTML & you're almost set: 

<div id="tuna-fish-sandwich"></div>

<script src="http://sled.io/rig.js"></script>

Now, if you check under the hood — the rig.js file has authentication server URL as the configuration parameters in it. If you're awesome, go ahead and save the JS locally and change the auth-server URL from galaxoid.sled.io to nebular.sled.io — et voila! You have a separate authentication server in use.

Rather want to use http://127.0.0.1:8000 as the authentication server? Guess what, you can totally do that! It's pretty straight-forward, but a fistbump for good luck anyways...

Why does it matter?

With all those identity solutions out there, it's a no-brainer that there has to be a problem; and as new solutions keeps coming in as time passes by, that means the problem still persists; and by their innumerable counts, it's reasonable to infer none of them solves the "problem" once and for all.

This is where Sled.IO kicks in..

No, it doesn't challenge any particular authentication mechanism. Neither does it set out to create a new best-of-all standard. Rather, it wraps 'em all up, allowing the developers to give support for all federated logins in their apps, and leaving it up to the users to take their pick.

Sled.IO sets itself as the "one thing to rule them all".
